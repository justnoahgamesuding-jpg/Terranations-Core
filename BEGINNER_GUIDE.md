# Beginner Guide

This guide is for new players joining the Terra server for the first time. It explains what the server is about, what you should do first, and which commands matter to normal players.

## What This Server Is

Terra is not a normal survival server.

The server is built around:

- jobs and progression
- countries and territory
- realistic farming and climate
- mining and building with support/stability rules
- slower, more deliberate development instead of fast strip-mining and instant automation

The goal is to build a world where players have to specialize, cooperate, plan settlements, and think about how land is used.

## The Main Idea

You do not start as a player who can do everything well.

You pick a job, level it up, and work with other players:

- `Miner` for mining and excavation
- `Lumberjack` for trees and wood gathering
- `Farmer` for crops, farmland, and food
- `Builder` for key building blocks and construction progression
- `Blacksmith` for forge-based crafting and metal work

You will usually also want to join a country, because countries give structure to territory, farms, settlement building, and group play.

## What To Do First

1. Choose your first job.
   Use `/jobs` or `/jobs open`.
2. Read your current job info.
   Use `/jobs info`.
3. Join a country if one is open.
   Use `/country list` and then `/country join <country name>`.
4. Start doing the work that matches your job.
5. Pay attention to climate, farming limits, and unstable terrain.

## Core Player Commands

### Jobs

- `/jobs`
  Opens the jobs GUI.
- `/jobs open`
  Same as `/jobs`.
- `/jobs info`
  Shows your current, primary, and secondary job info.
- `/jobs switch <job>`
  Switches to another job you already own.

Examples:

- `/jobs`
- `/jobs info`
- `/jobs switch farmer`

### Countries

- `/country`
  Opens the country menu/dashboard.
- `/country list`
  Opens the list of countries.
- `/country join <country name>`
  Joins an open country.
- `/country home`
  Teleports to your country home if your country has one set.
- `/country info`
  Shows information about the country at your location or your own context.
- `/country farmland`
  Shows current farmland usage for the current country area.
- `/country leave`
  Leaves your current country.

Examples:

- `/country list`
- `/country join Arcadia`
- `/country home`

### Chat

- `/cc`
  Toggles country chat on or off.
- `/cc on`
  Forces country chat on.
- `/cc off`
  Forces country chat off.
- `/gc`
  Toggles whether you can use global chat with `!message`.

Examples:

- `/cc`
- `/gc`
- `!Anyone selling bread?`

## How Progression Works

- You level your job by doing job-related actions.
- Some actions are blocked until your active job allows them.
- Some tools, crops, building blocks, or crafting paths are tied to specific jobs.
- Later on, you can unlock a second job.

This means your early-game choices matter.

If you pick `Farmer`, you should spend your first time farming, preparing land, planting, harvesting, and crafting food.

If you pick `Miner`, you should focus on controlled mining and resource extraction.

## Farming, Climate, and Crops

Farming on this server is not just "plant anywhere and wait."

Crop and sapling growth depends on:

- temperature
- season
- rain
- altitude
- the local climate

Important rules:

- Some crops and saplings grow best in specific temperatures and seasons.
- Rain can help growth for a while after it stops.
- Very cold areas can freeze water.
- Higher altitude is colder.
- Going too high or too low compared to the optimal altitude reduces growth.

If you are a farmer, climate matters a lot.

## Mining, Building, Supports, and Cave-Ins

Mining and building are not standard vanilla behavior.

Loose or weak terrain can collapse if it is not supported properly, and large player-built structures are expected to make structural sense.

This especially matters for:

- dirt
- grass
- coarse dirt
- sand
- gravel
- soft and fragile roof areas
- tall stone or cobblestone walls
- heavy stacked columns
- long unsupported roofs, bridges, docks, and overhangs

To make a safe mine or quarry, use supports such as:

- logs
- wood pillars
- fences
- fence gates
- walls
- braces and framed posts

The idea is simple:

- unsupported digging is risky
- unrealistic heavy structures are risky
- proper mine structure is safer
- proper building support is safer
- strip-mining and careless shaft digging are discouraged

If you make a large unsupported opening, or try to build tall or heavy structures without support, the area may collapse.

There is also a hidden stability meter while you build or break real structural blocks.

- It fills from valid block actions.
- When it fills, you temporarily get Terra stability debug vision.
- If you want to see the meter progress in chat, use `/terra stability meter on`.

## Starter Kit And Soulbound Items

Some starter items are soulbound.

That means:

- you cannot drop them
- you cannot move them into chests or other containers

These are meant to stay with the player they were given to.

## Good First Goals

A strong first session usually looks like this:

1. Pick a job.
2. Join a country.
3. Find out what your country needs.
4. Start doing the work that matches your job.
5. Build carefully instead of rushing.
6. Learn the local climate before making a large farm.

## Tips By Job

### Farmer

- Start near a good farming area.
- Watch crop growth speed.
- Use rainfall and climate to your advantage.
- Food crafting also helps your progression.

### Miner

- Do not dig big unsupported holes.
- Use supports early.
- Think in terms of mines and quarries, not just strip-mines.

### Lumberjack

- Replant consistently.
- Climate also matters for saplings.

### Builder

- Your progression is tied to construction-focused placement.
- Work with countries that are actively building.
- Expect large builds to need real support logic such as frames, posts, buttresses, and foundations.

### Blacksmith

- Expect forge-focused progression.
- Work with miners and farmers for supply chains.

## If You Feel Stuck

Use these first:

- `/jobs`
- `/jobs info`
- `/country`
- `/country list`
- `/country info`

If you still do not know what to do, ask your country members in chat or ask staff what job your country needs most right now.

## Short Version

If you only read one section, read this:

1. Pick a job with `/jobs`.
2. Join a country with `/country list` and `/country join <name>`.
3. Play in a way that matches your job.
4. Respect climate for farming.
5. Respect support and stability when mining, digging, and building.
6. Use `/terra stability meter on` if you want feedback while learning safe building patterns.
7. Build with planning, not like normal fast survival.
